Guidelines

Rule of thumb: Grasswire is based on facts.

Grasswire must be neutral, and the information gathering done on Grasswire must be done from a neutral point of view.

What to Submit/Upvote

Not all users will see submission options, as there is a karma threshold.

On-Topic: Anything newsworthy - information about what is transpiring in the world based on factual evidence. The more something affects the world population, the more newsworthy it is.

Off-Topic: Opinion, speculation, political analysis, political cartoons, celebrity gossip. If you see these submissions, flag them. (Flagging is also subject to a karma threshold.)

Fact-Checks

Fact-checks rely on facts.

What is a fact?

Facts cannot be wrong. If something is wrong it is our understanding of the facts, not the facts themesleves.

Facts do not change based on one's opinion or political persuasion.

How to gather facts

  • The best sources for facts are those on the ground.
  • Media outlets get it wrong all the time, especially as news is breaking.
  • One bad source can be used by multiple media outlets. Find the root of the information to determine if a source is accurate.
  • Which media outlet something appeared on is neither proof of its truthfulness or untruthfulness. "This originated on [Fox News/MSNBC/SomeSource], which we all know to be [accurate/inaccurate/good/bad]" is rarely a valid argument. If it's incorrect, point to the fact that is incorrect, not the bias of the outlet.
  • "We're getting reports" means nothing.
  • Often times not all of the information to determine whether something is accurate is available. In those cases, one must either find a way to get the information or wait. Anything else is speculation.

Comments

Don't say things you wouldn't say in a face to face conversation.

Some words have become "loaded" and rarely contribute anything of value, unless used in their most literal sense. Some examples are "socialist," "fascist", "Nazi," "dictator" "lefty/righty" etc. Don't use these words except in their most literal sense.

When disagreeing, please reply to the argument instead of calling names. E.g. "That is an idiotic thing to say; 1 + 1 is 2, not 3" can be shortened to "1 + 1 is 2, not 3."

Please don't sign comments, especially with your url. They're already signed with your username.

Please don't use uppercase for emphasis.
